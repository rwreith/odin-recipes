Fettuccine Alfredo

Description

This homemade alfredo sauce is made with simple ingredients -- butter, parmesan cheese, garlic, heavy cream, and salt. It's a 5-ingredient alfredo sauce that is beyond easy!

This homemade alfredo sauce can be tossed with the pasta so the pasta soaks up all of the sauce or it can be spooned over hot, fresh pasta. I love to use fresh refrigerated fettuccine noodles because it tastes that much better. If you want the fettuccine alfredo to be a little more "saucy" toss the sauce with less than one pound of pasta (you may start with 12 ounces of fettuccine).

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound Fettuccine
  • 6 tablespoons Butter
  • 3 minced Garlic Cloves
  • 12 ounces Heavy Cream
  • 1/4 teaspoon Salt
  • 1 1/4 cup shredded Parmesan Cheese
  • 1/4 teaspoon Pepper
  • 2 tablespoons Italian Parsely

Directions:

  1. In a large pot, heat water over a high heat until boiling. Add salt to season the water. Once boiling, add fettuccine and cook according to package instructions.
  2. In a large skillet or pan, heat butter over medium heat. Add minced garlic and cook for 1 to 2 minutes. Stir in heavy cream.
  3. Let heavy cream reduce and cook for 5 to 8 minutes. Add half of the parmesan cheese to the mixture and whick well until smooth. Keep over heat and whisk well until cheese is melted.
  4. Save some pasta water. The pasta water is full of flavor and can be used to this out the sauce if needed.
  5. Toss alfredo sauce with the fettuccine pasta and add half the parmesan cheese. Once tossed, garnish with the remaining cheese. Add a little pasta water if sauce needs to be thinned out.
  6. Garnish with Italian Parsley.
